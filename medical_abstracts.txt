Title: Pembrolizumab plus Chemotherapy in Advanced Non-Small-Cell Lung Cancer
Abstract: In this double-blind, phase 3 trial, we randomly assigned patients with previously untreated metastatic non-small-cell lung cancer (NSCLC) without EGFR or ALK mutations to receive pembrolizumab plus chemotherapy or placebo plus chemotherapy. The primary end points were overall survival and progression-free survival. After a median follow-up of 10.5 months, the hazard ratio for death in the pembrolizumab-combination group was 0.49 (95% CI, 0.38 to 0.64; P<0.001) compared to the placebo group. Progression-free survival was significantly longer in the pembrolizumab-combination group (median, 8.8 months vs. 4.9 months; HR, 0.52; P<0.001). Adverse events of grade 3 or higher occurred in 67.2% of patients in the pembrolizumab-combination group. We conclude that the addition of pembrolizumab to standard chemotherapy significantly prolongs overall survival and progression-free survival in patients with metastatic NSCLC.

Title: Semaglutide and Cardiovascular Outcomes in Patients with Overweight or Obesity
Abstract: This multicenter, double-blind, randomized trial evaluated the cardiovascular efficacy of semaglutide (2.4 mg subcutaneously once weekly) versus placebo in adults aged 45 years or older with pre-existing cardiovascular disease and a body-mass index of 27 or higher, but without diabetes. The primary safety and efficacy composite end point was death from cardiovascular causes, nonfatal myocardial infarction, or nonfatal stroke. A total of 17,604 patients underwent randomization. Over a mean follow-up of 39.8 months, the primary composite end point occurred in 6.5% of patients in the semaglutide group and in 8.0% of patients in the placebo group (hazard ratio, 0.80; 95% CI, 0.72 to 0.90; P<0.001). Semaglutide once weekly was associated with a 20% reduction in major adverse cardiovascular events (MACE) in this population.

Title: Lecanemab in Patients with Early Alzheimer's Disease
Abstract: Lecanemab, a humanized IgG1 monoclonal antibody that binds with high affinity to amyloid-beta soluble protofibrils, was evaluated in a phase 3, multicenter, double-blind trial involving 1795 patients aged 50 to 90 years with early Alzheimer's disease. Patients were randomly assigned to receive intravenous lecanemab (10 mg per kilogram of body weight every 2 weeks) or placebo. The primary end point was the change from baseline at 18 months in the score on the Clinical Dementia Rating-Sum of Boxes (CDR-SB). At 18 months, the mean change in the CDR-SB score was 1.21 in the lecanemab group and 1.66 in the placebo group, representing a difference of -0.45 (P<0.001; a 27% slowing of clinical decline). Amyloid plaque burden was significantly reduced in the lecanemab group. Infusion-related reactions occurred in 26.4% of lecanemab patients, and amyloid-related imaging abnormalities (ARIA) occurred in 12.6%.

Title: Trastuzumab Deruxtecan in HER2-Low Advanced Breast Cancer
Abstract: In this phase 3 trial, we evaluated trastuzumab deruxtecan, an antibody-drug conjugate, against physician's choice of chemotherapy in patients with HER2-low (immunohistochemistry score of 1+ or 2+ with negative in situ hybridization) metastatic breast cancer who had received prior chemotherapy. A total of 557 patients were randomized in a 2:1 ratio. The primary end point was progression-free survival in the hormone-receptor-positive cohort. Among hormone-receptor-positive patients, the median progression-free survival was 10.1 months in the trastuzumab deruxtecan group compared to 5.4 months in the chemotherapy group (hazard ratio for disease progression or death, 0.51; P<0.001). Overall survival was also significantly longer (23.9 months vs. 17.5 months; HR, 0.64; P=0.003). The most common grade 3 or higher adverse event was neutropenia.

Title: Empagliflozin in Heart Failure with Preserved Ejection Fraction
Abstract: The sodium-glucose cotransporter 2 (SGLT2) inhibitor empagliflozin has been shown to reduce cardiovascular risk in patients with heart failure and a reduced ejection fraction. In this double-blind trial, we randomized 5988 patients with class II-IV heart failure and an ejection fraction of more than 40% to receive empagliflozin (10 mg once daily) or placebo. The primary outcome was a composite of cardiovascular death or hospitalization for heart failure. Over a median of 26.2 months, the primary outcome occurred in 13.8% of patients in the empagliflozin group and in 17.1% of patients in the placebo group (hazard ratio, 0.79; 95% CI, 0.69 to 0.90; P<0.001). The effect was mainly driven by a 29% lower risk of hospitalization for heart failure. Serious adverse events were reported in 47.9% of the empagliflozin group.

Title: Gene Therapy with Onasemnogene Abeparvovec for Spinal Muscular Atrophy
Abstract: Spinal muscular atrophy (SMA) is a progressive neuromuscular disease caused by mutations in the SMN1 gene. In a phase 3 open-label trial, we evaluated the safety and efficacy of a single intravenous infusion of onasemnogene abeparvovec (an adeno-associated virus vector-based gene therapy delivering a functional copy of the SMN gene) in symptomatic infants with SMA type 1 who were younger than 6 months at the time of vector infusion. The primary efficacy end points were survival at 14 months and the ability to sit independently for at least 30 seconds at 18 months. All 22 enrolled patients survived to 14 months without permanent ventilation, and 13 patients (59%) achieved independent sitting at 18 months. Transient elevation of aminotransferase levels was the most frequent adverse event, reflecting mild hepatic injury.

Title: Efficacy and Safety of Dupilumab in Moderate-to-Severe Atopic Dermatitis
Abstract: Dupilumab is a fully human monoclonal antibody that blocks interleukin-4 and interleukin-13, key drivers of type 2 helper T-cell-mediated inflammation. In two randomized, double-blind, phase 3 trials (SOLO 1 and SOLO 2), we evaluated dupilumab monotherapy in adults with moderate-to-severe atopic dermatitis that was inadequately controlled by topical treatment. Patients were randomized in a 1:1:1 ratio to receive subcutaneous dupilumab (300 mg weekly or every 2 weeks) or placebo for 16 weeks. The primary outcome was the proportion of patients who achieved an Investigator's Global Assessment (IGA) score of 0 or 1 (clear or almost clear) and a reduction of 2 or more points from baseline. In SOLO 1, the IGA endpoint was met by 38% (weekly) and 37% (every 2 weeks) of dupilumab patients versus 10% of placebo patients (P<0.001). Results in SOLO 2 were similar, demonstrating rapid and significant clearing of skin lesions.

Title: CAR T-Cell Therapy Tisagenlecleucel in Pediatric B-Cell Leukemia
Abstract: In this single-cohort, phase 2, multicenter study, we evaluated the efficacy and safety of tisagenlecleucel, an anti-CD19 chimeric antigen receptor (CAR) T-cell therapy, in pediatric and young adult patients with refractory or relapsed B-cell acute lymphoblastic leukemia (ALL). A total of 75 patients received an infusion of tisagenlecleucel. The primary end point was the overall remission rate within 3 months, defined as complete remission or complete remission with incomplete hematologic recovery. The overall remission rate was 81% (95% CI, 71 to 89), and all patients who had a response were negative for minimal residual disease. The rate of event-free survival was 73% at 6 months and 50% at 12 months. Cytokine release syndrome occurred in 77% of patients, and 47% required intensive care management for hemodynamic or respiratory support.

Title: Dual Antiplatelet Therapy post Stroke and Transient Ischemic Attack
Abstract: The efficacy of dual antiplatelet therapy (DAPT) with clopidogrel and aspirin compared to aspirin alone in preventing recurrent ischemic stroke remains controversial. In this randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial, we enrolled patients within 24 hours of an acute minor ischemic stroke or high-risk transient ischemic attack (TIA). Patients were assigned to receive DAPT (clopidogrel 300 mg loading dose, followed by 75 mg daily, plus aspirin 75-300 mg on day 1, followed by 75 mg daily) or aspirin alone (plus placebo clopidogrel) for 90 days. The primary efficacy outcome was recurrent stroke during 90 days. Recurrent stroke occurred in 8.2% of patients in the DAPT group compared to 11.7% of patients in the aspirin-alone group (hazard ratio, 0.68; P<0.001). Moderate-to-severe bleeding occurred in 0.3% of both groups.

Title: Baricitinib in Hospitalized Patients with Severe Covid-19 Infection
Abstract: Baricitinib, an oral selective Janus kinase (JAK) 1 and 2 inhibitor, targets the inflammatory cytokine cascade. In this randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, phase 3 trial, we evaluated baricitinib (4 mg daily for up to 14 days) plus standard of care (including systemic corticosteroids and remdesivir) in hospitalized adults with Covid-19 who required supplemental oxygen. The primary end point was progression to non-invasive ventilation, high-flow oxygen, invasive mechanical ventilation, or death. A total of 1525 patients were randomized. Progression to mechanical ventilation or death occurred in 27.8% of patients in the baricitinib group versus 30.5% in the placebo group (hazard ratio, 0.85; P=0.09). However, 28-day all-cause mortality was significantly lower with baricitinib (8% vs 13%; hazard ratio, 0.57; P=0.0018), demonstrating a key survival benefit.

Title: Pembrolizumab como terapia adyuvante en melanoma resecado de alto riesgo
Abstract: En este estudio aleatorizado y doble ciego de fase 3, evaluamos la eficacia del anticuerpo monoclonal anti-PD-1 pembrolizumab como terapia adyuvante en comparación con placebo en pacientes con melanoma en estadio III que había sido completamente resecado quirúrgicamente. Un total de 1019 pacientes fueron asignados aleatoriamente para recibir pembrolizumab (200 mg por vía intravenosa) o placebo cada 3 semanas durante un año. El criterio de valoración principal fue la supervivencia libre de recurrencia. A los 18 meses de seguimiento, la tasa de supervivencia libre de recurrencia fue del 75.4% en el grupo de pembrolizumab y del 61.0% en el grupo de placebo (hazard ratio para recurrencia o muerte, 0.57; P<0.001). Los eventos adversos relacionados con el sistema inmunitario de grado 3 o superior ocurrieron en el 14.3% de los pacientes tratados con pembrolizumab.

Title: Donepezilo en el tratamiento de la demencia vascular moderada
Abstract: Evaluamos la eficacia y seguridad del inhibidor de la acetilcolinesterasa donepezilo en un ensayo clínico aleatorizado, doble ciego y controlado con placebo de 24 semanas de duración en pacientes con demencia vascular probable de intensidad leve a moderada según los criterios NINDS-AIREN. Los pacientes recibieron donepezilo (5 mg o 10 mg una vez al día) o placebo. El criterio de valoración principal de eficacia cognitiva se evaluó mediante la escala ADAS-cog. Los pacientes del grupo de donepezilo de 10 mg mostraron una mejoría significativa en la puntuación ADAS-cog en comparación con el grupo de placebo (diferencia de la media de mínimos cuadrados, -1.65; P=0.002). El donepezilo de 5 mg también mostró mejoría cognitiva frente a placebo. Las reacciones adversas más comunes fueron diarrea, náuseas y calambres musculares.
